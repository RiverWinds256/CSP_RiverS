Canyonlands

Canyonlands is a beautiful national park located in southeastern Utah near moab. With many acres of picturesque land, vibrant canyons, arches, buttes and much more, Canyonlands is a memorable place to visit. The park has nearly 337,598 acres of eye-catching landscapes, and plenty of recreational activities for the whole family.


Activities for the adventurous

  • River Rafting on Cataract Canyon, a rapid and intense ride (River permit required)
  • Rock Climbing in islands of the sky
  • The needles district, rugged terrain perfect for experienced backpackers and hikers

For the whole family

  • Various hiking trails varying in difficulty
  • Stargazing
  • chin scatches
  • Scenic drives such as the Shafer trail

Things to keep in mind

Due to the popularity of this location, there will be crowds at certain locations, inferring with activities of viewpoints. Be aware when choosing a time to visit if everything will be overcrowded.

Weather can be unpredictable, with flash floods and studden storms appearing. Keep an eye on the forecast before planning a trip or activity in case of severe weather.

Only certain areas are accessible to wheelchairs, and depending on location may be completely inaccessible. The “Island in the sky" section is completely accessible, while “The Needles” have little to offer and “The Maze” is completely inaccessible. If visiting in a wheelchair or with somebody in a wheelchair make sure to research.
